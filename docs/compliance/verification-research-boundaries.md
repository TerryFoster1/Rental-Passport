# Verification Research Boundaries

Date: 2026-07-23  
Status: compliance guardrails for product, operations, and engineering.

## Purpose

Rental Passport needs research and verification operations without becoming an unbounded investigation product. This document defines what reviewers, automations, and future AI tooling may and may not do.

## Allowed Research

- Confirm employer domain, business name, and contact plausibility.
- Confirm property manager or landlord contact details supplied by the tenant.
- Review tenant-provided documents for consistency.
- Verify report metadata supplied by a tenant or provider.
- Request additional information from the tenant when evidence is incomplete.
- Record only facts needed for the stated verification purpose.

## Restricted Research

Do not perform broad background investigation unless a future legal/compliance review explicitly approves it.

Restricted areas include:

- Criminal background checks.
- Social media searches.
- Open-ended internet reputation checks.
- Tenant blacklists.
- Protected-class inference.
- Unapproved court/tribunal search products.
- Automated fraud conclusions shown to landlords.

## OpenRoom and Tribunal Records

OpenRoom or tribunal-record searches are landlord-facing add-ons only if legally approved and built inside the correct landlord review context. They must not appear in tenant pricing, tenant onboarding, or tenant passport marketing.

## Adverse Information

If verification uncovers conflicting or adverse information:

- Record the factual inconsistency.
- Give the tenant a chance to correct or explain when appropriate.
- Avoid labels like "bad tenant", "high risk", or "fraud" in landlord-facing summaries.
- Escalate legal/compliance-sensitive cases before finalizing any external-facing status.

## Marketing Boundary

Marketing may say Rental Passport verifies information. Marketing must not imply that Rental Passport approves, recommends, ranks, guarantees, screens, or underwrites tenants.
