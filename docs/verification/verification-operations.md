# Verification Operations Manual

This manual defines internal reviewer workflows for the manual-first MVP.

## Operating Principles

- Review evidence, not people.
- Record facts, not opinions.
- Keep sensitive documents private.
- Use AI only as reviewer assistance.
- Make every decision auditable.
- Escalate uncertainty instead of guessing.

## Review Queues

Initial queues:

- Identity Review
- Employment Review
- Income Review
- Credit Review
- Rental History Review
- Reference Review
- Fraud Review
- More Information Requested
- Escalated Compliance Review

## Standard Case Workflow

1. Case enters queue.
2. Reviewer claims or is assigned the case.
3. Reviewer confirms tenant consent and passport version.
4. Reviewer inspects evidence.
5. Reviewer records evidence quality and confidence.
6. Reviewer chooses status.
7. Reviewer writes internal notes.
8. Reviewer writes tenant-facing or landlord-facing summary where required.
9. Reviewer sets expiry or reverification requirement.
10. Decision is saved to audit history.

## Identity Review

Evidence:

- Front of government ID
- Back of government ID
- Selfie

Checks:

- ID name
- Date of birth
- ID expiry
- ID photo and selfie consistency
- Document clarity
- Signs of alteration
- Duplicate identity concerns

MVP SLA: identity review is usually completed within 24 hours.

## Employment Review

Evidence may include:

- Employer confirmation through secure link
- Company email domain
- Employer website
- Pay stub
- Employment letter
- Optional bank deposit proof

Reviewer checks consistency across employer name, domain, position, dates, income, document names, and tenant profile.

## Income Review

Evidence may include pay stubs, employment verification, tax documents, government income statements, gig income records, self-employed income records, or optional bank deposit proof.

Reviewer records verified monthly income, verified annual income, method, period covered, and income multiple when advertised rent is known.

## Credit Review

MVP methods:

- Tenant uploads recent credit report.
- Tenant authorizes manual provider-assisted report through legally permitted services such as SingleKey or FrontLobby.

Reviewer records provider/source, report date, score, score range/status, collections, public records, bankruptcy, consumer proposal, payment history summary, and whether report was uploaded or sourced through Rental Passport.

## Rental History Review

Evidence may include past address, lease, landlord contact, property manager contact, move-in date, move-out date, reason for leaving, and optional payment proof.

Reviewer answers:

- Was this tenancy real?
- Did the landlord or property manager confirm it?
- Were issues reported?
- Would the previous landlord rent to them again?

## Reference Review

Reviewer records:

- Contacted
- Responded
- Phone or email works
- Relationship declared
- Response captured
- Response date

Avoid reference analytics that do not help landlord trust.

## Fraud Review

Fraud review handles suspected altered documents, fake employers, suspicious references, duplicate identities, inconsistent addresses, suspicious domains, IP or device anomalies, and repeated evidence across passports.

Outcomes:

- No issue detected
- Requires additional evidence
- Requires manual review
- Unable to verify
- Confirmed mismatch
- Confirmed duplicate
- Revoked verification

## Requests For More Information

Requests must be specific, respectful, and explain what is needed to verify the section. Do not ask for bank statements or highly sensitive evidence unless necessary for confidence and legally appropriate.

## Approval And Rejection

Internal review may approve or reject a verification status. It must not approve or reject an applicant for housing.

## Escalation

Escalate to compliance or senior review when:

- Evidence suggests fraud
- Identity appears duplicated
- Jurisdictional rule is unclear
- Credit authorization is unclear
- Document handling risk is high
- AI and reviewer assessments conflict

## Quality Assurance

QA should sample reviewer decisions for consistency, evidence quality, note clarity, expiry accuracy, legal compliance, and proper separation of internal-only notes from landlord-facing summaries.

## Future Automation

Future automation may assist triage, extract document fields, flag inconsistencies, summarize responses, and prioritize queues. Automation must remain auditable and reviewable.
